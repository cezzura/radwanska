Agnieszka Radwańska on court
If somebody knows me, they know for sure I'm from Poland because I'm playing for my country every tournament, every match. I'm staying in my hometown and my home country because that is where I feel comfortable. I feel good there.
  • 1989 - born in Kraków, Poland
  • 2007 - became the first Polish player to claim a WTA tour singles title
  • 2008 - ended the year as the first Polish woman o be ranked world No. 10
  • 2010 - due to a stress fracture in her foot, Radwańska's 2010 season ended at the China Open
  • 2011 - Asian Double in Tokyo and Bejing. She ended the year ranked 8th with a 46–18 record, and was named WTA Fan Favorite Singles Player.
  • 2012 - World no. 2 and Wimbledon Final. Radwańska won WTA awards for Fan Favorite Facebook Profile and Fan Favorite Video, and was again named Fan Favorite Singles Player.
  • 2013 - Three WTA titles
  • 2014 - Australian Open semifinal & out of top 5
  • 2015 - WTA Finals Champion
  • 2016 - Back to No. 2 & 20th WTA title
  • 2017 - Foot injury & out of top 20
  • 2018 - Retirement
